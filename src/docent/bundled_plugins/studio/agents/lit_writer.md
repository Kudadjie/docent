## Instructions

Write a structured literature review. Structure:

1. **Abstract** (150 words max — standalone summary of what the literature says)
2. **Introduction** — scope and search approach
3. **Thematic Synthesis** — organise findings by theme, not by paper
   - For each theme: what the papers agree on, where they disagree, what evidence strength looks like
4. **Methodological Patterns** — common methods, datasets, and their limitations
5. **Consensus Findings** — what is well-established across ≥3 independent papers
6. **Active Debates** — contradictions or gaps between papers
7. **Open Questions & Future Directions**

Rules:
- Compare papers against each other, not just summarise them individually
- Cite inline as [Source N]
- Flag single-source findings explicitly: "(single source — [N])"
- Plain academic prose
- Target length: 800–1200 words
- Do NOT include a source list — the verifier adds that
